<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Magikat and Accessibility Features">

Recognizing that physics-based games can be frustrating, the developers implemented the Magikat system. This feature acts as a safety net, ensuring that the **heave ho 2 difficulty** remains accessible to players of all skill levels, including children or those new to the genre.

When the game detects a player is struggling—usually after multiple deaths in the same section—Magikat appears to offer a "floaty." This floaty allows the player to hover or move more freely, bypassing the most punishing physics requirements of a specific jump.

| Feature | Function | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Magikat Floaty** | Provides a temporary flight/hover ability | Beginners / Stuck players |
| **Checkpoint System** | Frequent resets at the start of small sections | Casual play |
| **Relief Drone** | Allows one skilled player to carry others | Mixed-skill groups |
| **Tutorial Mode** | Dedicated space to practice basic swings | First-time players |

<Callout type="success" title="Success Strategy">
If you are playing with a mixed-skill group, let the most experienced player handle the "Relief Drone" joystick. This allows the less experienced players to simply hold on while the pilot navigates the hazards.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Mastering Complex Swings">

To overcome the mid-game difficulty spikes, players must move beyond simple "left-to-right" swings. Mastering the human pendulum is the only way to clear the massive gaps found in later worlds.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Anchor the Chain">
    The strongest player (or the one with the best position) must grip a solid surface with both hands. This player acts as the "anchor" for the rest of the team.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Link">
    The second player grabs the feet or hands of the anchor. Subsequent players join the chain until the "tail" player is free to swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Momentum">
    The tail player moves the joystick left and right in rhythm. The anchor player can assist by slightly shifting their weight, though they must never release their grip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Calculated Release">
    At the peak of the arc, the player connecting the swinging group to the anchor must release. Timing is critical; releasing too early or too late will result in a vertical drop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Technical Note">
In the 2026 version, the physics engine is more sensitive to the weight of the players. A four-player chain has significantly more inertia than a two-player chain, making it harder to start the swing but providing much more distance once momentum is built.
</Callout>

</Section>
