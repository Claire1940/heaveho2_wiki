<Section icon={Swords} title="Master the Controls and Physics Mechanics">

To understand **heave ho 2 how to play**, you must first master the unique control scheme. Unlike traditional platformers, you do not control feet or jumping. Instead, you control two hands independently. You use the analog stick to rotate your arms and the left and right triggers to grab surfaces with the corresponding hands. Releasing a trigger at the peak of a swing launches your character through the air.

| Control Input | Controller Action | Keyboard Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Left Grip | Left Trigger (LT / L2) | Left Shift or A Key |
| Right Grip | Right Trigger (RT / R2) | Right Shift or D Key |
| Arm Rotation | Left Analog Stick | Arrow Keys or Mouse |
| Quick Reset | Y Button or Triangle | R Key |

Managing momentum is the core mechanic of the gameplay loop. When you hang from a single hand, you can rotate the analog stick to swing back and forth. The wider the arc of your swing, the more velocity you gather. If you release your grip at the correct angle, that velocity translates into a projectile trajectory, sending you flying toward the next platform.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Co-Op Strategies">

Cooperative play is where the game truly shines, but it also introduces the highest potential for failure. When playing with others, you can grab onto your teammates' hands, feet, or bodies to form human chains. This allows you to cross massive gaps that would be impossible to clear individually.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Anchor the Lead Player">
    One player must firmly grip a solid wall or platform. This player acts as the anchor and must not release their grip under any circumstances during the swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Human Chain">
    The second player grabs the free hand or body of the anchor player. Additional players can attach themselves to the bottom of the chain to extend its reach.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Swing Momentum">
    The lowest player in the chain rotates their analog stick to swing the entire chain back and forth, gradually building momentum.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Launch and Grab">
    At the peak of the swing, the lowest player releases their grip on the chain (or the anchor launches the entire group) to reach the destination platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Communication is vital when executing these steps. A simple countdown helps ensure that everyone releases their grips at the correct moment, preventing players from dropping into the abyss.

| Hazard Type | Danger Level | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Spikes | High | Swing over them using a two-player chain |
| Electricity | High | Avoid contact and wait for the cycle to end |
| Wind Currents | Medium | Use heavy momentum to cut through the draft |
| Moving Platforms | Medium | Time your launch to match the platform path |

<Callout type="success" title="Chain Safety">
When forming a chain, the player at the top should always keep both hands anchored if possible to provide maximum stability for the players below.
</Callout>
