<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Progression & Teamwork Steps">

Starting your journey in Heave Ho 2 requires a complete shift in how you think about platforming. Unlike traditional games where you jump with a single button, here you control two independent hands. Success depends entirely on building human chains, maintaining anchors, and communicating with your teammates to ensure no one falls into the abyss.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master Independent Hand Controls">
    Your character consists of a head and two long elastic arms. Practice holding a surface with one hand using the corresponding trigger while rotating your body to reach for the next grip point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish a Secure Anchor">
    Before any teammate attempts a risky jump, ensure the rear player is firmly anchored to a solid platform. This player should remain completely still to stabilize the rest of the chain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Form a Human Chain">
    Extend your reach by grabbing a teammate's hand, head, or body. Chains allow the team to span wide gaps and reach distant platforms that are impossible to clear individually.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Build Rotational Momentum">
    To cross massive voids, all connected players must rotate their analog sticks in unison. This synchronized movement builds momentum, allowing the front player to swing outward at high speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Coordinate the Release">
    The anchor player must call out the release timing. Release only the hand connecting you to the chain, keeping your free hand extended to immediately grip the destination platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Double Releases">
A common beginner mistake is releasing both grip buttons simultaneously. Always verify which hand is holding your teammate before letting go of a trigger.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Controls & Advanced Swinging Techniques">

To move efficiently, you must master the control layout and translate basic inputs into advanced movement techniques. Hand-over-hand climbing, throwing teammates, and performing last-second catches are essential skills for clearing the later campaign worlds.

| Action | Controller Input | Mechanics Tip |
| Move and Rotate | Left Analog Stick | Use small circular motions for precision climbing. |
| Grip Left Hand | Left Trigger / L Button | Hold to anchor; release to swing or transfer. |
| Grip Right Hand | Right Trigger / R Button | Use as a guide hand while keeping left hand anchored. |
| Climb Walls | Alternate Left & Right Grips | Move hand-over-hand; never release both at once. |
| Throw Ally | Grip, build momentum, release | The thrown player must point their hand toward the target. |
| Catch Ally | Position open hand, hold trigger | Open your catching hand early; do not chase the fall. |

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Momentum Building">
    Rotate your body in small, controlled circles to build speed before expanding into larger swings. This prevents chaotic shaking and ensures a predictable launch trajectory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Safe Handoffs">
    When carrying objective items like keys or ingredients, pass them directly from hand to hand rather than throwing them across gaps to minimize the risk of dropping them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aerial Catches">
    If a teammate misses a jump, remain anchored and extend your free arm directly into their flight path. Hold the grip trigger as soon as their body collides with your hand.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip" title="Precision Landing">
When landing on small or narrow platforms, approach hand-first and grip immediately. Landing head-first causes your character to bounce off, often leading to a fatal fall.
</Callout>
