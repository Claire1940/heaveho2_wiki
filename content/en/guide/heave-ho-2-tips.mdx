Heave Ho 2 elevates the physics-based chaos of its predecessor by introducing more complex environmental interactions and refined control schemes. Mastering **heave ho 2 tips** starts with understanding that your character's arms operate independently. Each analog stick controls a corresponding arm, and each trigger (L2/R2 or LT/RT) controls the grip of that hand. This separation is the foundation of every advanced maneuver in the game.

To effectively navigate the early levels, players must internalize the weight and momentum of their characters. Unlike standard platformers, movement is entirely dependent on friction and grip. If you let go too early, physics will carry you in an unintended arc.

<Callout type="tip" title="Grip Stability">
When hanging from a single point, your character will rotate. Use this rotation to build momentum. Grabbing with both hands simultaneously provides a "lock" state that stops rotation, allowing you to stabilize before a big jump.

<Section icon={Target} title="Solving Complex Puzzle Levels">

Heave Ho 2 introduces "Puzzle Levels" that require more than just swinging. These levels often involve finding specific items, such as keys or Tiki statues, and moving them to a designated slot. These puzzles test your ability to manipulate objects while maintaining your own grip on the environment.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Objective">
    Look for question mark icons or empty slots. In many levels, the "real" key is hidden among several fakes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Path">
    Move obstacles like stone blocks or swinging hammers out of the way before attempting to carry the objective item.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Form a Transport Chain">
    One player should focus on holding the item while others focus on moving that player. Trying to swing and hold an item simultaneously is significantly harder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Insertion">
    Bring the item to the slot. Some slots require a specific orientation (e.g., the Tiki head must be upright).
  </Step>
</Steps>

The "Key Puzzle" is a notorious challenge where players must pass a key through a series of narrow gaps. Using the "Fling" mechanic here is risky; instead, use a controlled "Hand-to-Hand" pass to ensure the key doesn't fall into an unreachable area.

| Puzzle Type | Key Mechanic | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Key & Lock** | Precision passing and grip management | Medium |
| **Tiki Arrangement** | Object orientation and heavy lifting | Hard |
| **Switch Sequences** | Simultaneous activation of multiple levers | Medium |
| **Hidden Path** | Environmental observation and blind jumps | Hard |

<Callout type="success" title="Puzzle Shortcut">
In puzzle levels with multiple keys, look for subtle visual cues like a slight glow or a different texture. These often indicate the "real" key, saving you time from testing every fake one.
</Callout>

<Section icon={ChefHat} title="Mastering the Cooking and Interaction Levels">

One of the most unique additions to the sequel is the "Chef Mode" levels. In these stages, players must assemble ingredients (like lettuce, cheese, and chicken) in a specific order to complete a recipe. This requires a high degree of spatial awareness and the ability to juggle multiple items without dropping them.

**Cooking Level Order of Operations:**
1. **Bottom Bun**: Secure the base on the preparation plate.
2. **Main Protein**: Fetch the chicken or patty and layer it on top.
3. **Condiments/Garnish**: Add lettuce, cheese, or sauce as requested.
4. **Top Bun**: Seal the sandwich.
5. **The Bell**: One player must reach and ring the service bell to finish the level.

| Ingredient | Weight/Physics | Handling Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Buns** | Light / High Friction | Easy |
| **Cheese/Lettuce** | Very Light / Floats | Medium |
| **Chicken/Patty** | Heavy / Slippery | Hard |
| **Sauce/Mustard** | Fluid / Messy | Very Hard |

<Callout type="info" title="Ingredient Management">
If an ingredient falls off the platform, it will usually respawn at its original location. Don't chase a falling lettuce leaf into the abyss; stay safe and wait for the respawn.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Rocket} title="Space Navigation and Zero-G Strategies">

The Space levels in Heave Ho 2 introduce zero-gravity mechanics and rocket ship piloting. These levels change the fundamental physics of the game, as you no longer have constant gravity pulling you down. Instead, you must use momentum and levers to navigate the cosmos.

**Space Station Pilot Guide:**
- **Left Lever**: Controls the left thruster (tilts the ship right).
- **Right Lever**: Controls the right thruster (tilts the ship left).
- **Bottom Lever**: Controls main propulsion.
- **Coordination**: Two players should handle the side levers while a third manages the main engine.

| Space Element | Physics Impact | Navigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zero-G** | Infinite momentum / No fall damage | Use walls to push off; avoid floating into open space |
| **Thruster Levers** | High torque / Rapid rotation | Use short, rhythmic pulls to maintain a straight path |
| **Asteroids** | Collision hazards | Grab onto them to stop unwanted momentum |
| **Airlocks** | Gated progression | Requires simultaneous lever pulls from two players |

<Callout type="tip" title="Zero-G Movement">
In zero-gravity sections, you can "swim" through the air by rapidly moving your arms in a circular motion. It's slow, but it can save you if you find yourself floating away from any grabable surfaces.
</Callout>
