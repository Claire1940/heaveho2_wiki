<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Mastering Physics and Momentum">

Understanding the physics engine is the first step to surviving the campaign. In **Heave Ho 2**, your character consists of a head and two long, elastic arms. You do not have a jump button; instead, you must use your hands to grip surfaces, swing your body, and launch yourself toward the next objective. The key to success is learning how to alternate your grips without letting go of your anchor point.

Cooperation is mandatory in the campaign. Most levels require at least two players to form a human chain to reach distant ledges. If one player falls, the entire chain is at risk unless a secure anchor is maintained.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Independent Hand Control">
    The left and right triggers (or mouse buttons) control the respective hands. Practice moving the analog stick to rotate your body while holding a surface with just one hand. This rotation is how you build the speed necessary for long jumps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Anchor Strategy">
    Always designate one player as the "Anchor." This player should keep at least one hand firmly gripped to a solid, non-moving platform. Only when the rest of the team has found a new grip should the anchor release and move forward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Building the Chain">
    To cross wide gaps, have the anchor hold the ledge while the second player grabs the anchor's feet or free hand. The third and fourth players continue this chain. The player at the very end of the chain will have the most momentum for the final swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Release Timing">
    Release the grip button at the apex of your swing. Releasing too early results in a vertical drop, while releasing too late will slam you back into the wall you just left. Timing is everything in the 72 co-op levels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Communication is Key">
When playing online, use a voice chat or the in-game "Ready" prompts. Counting down "3, 2, 1, Release!" significantly reduces the number of accidental team wipes.
</Callout>

</Section>
