<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering the Basics: Beginner Guide & Teamwork">

To survive the campaign's diverse obstacles, players must grasp the fundamental physics engine. Unlike traditional platformers, characters in Heave Ho 2 do not have legs. Instead, they consist of a round body and two extremely long, elastic arms controlled independently. 

The primary objective is simple: get every player from the starting platform to the checkered finish zone. However, doing so requires meticulous coordination, as levels are filled with spikes, moving platforms, and wide chasms that cannot be crossed alone.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn Your Two Hands">
    Understand that your left and right hands are bound to separate controller triggers. Practice grabbing a surface with one hand while reaching out toward the next grip point with the other.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Create a Reliable Anchor">
    Before attempting any team movement, designate one player as the anchor. This player must secure a firm grip on a solid, non-moving environmental surface and remain completely still.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build a Human Chain">
    Have consecutive players grab the hands, heads, or bodies of those ahead of them. This extends the group's reach across massive gaps, allowing the lead player to grab distant ledges.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Generate Swinging Momentum">
    Rotate your analog sticks in unison to swing the chain back and forth. Gradually build momentum rather than rushing a wild throw, as controlled swings are far safer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Release and Catch Safely">
    The lead player must extend a free hand toward the target. Once in range, the connecting player releases their grip, launching the leader. The leader must immediately squeeze the trigger to grab the new surface.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch Your Grip">
Releasing the wrong trigger is the most common beginner mistake. Always verify which hand is anchoring the team before letting go to prevent sending the entire group falling to their doom.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Controls & Advanced Swinging Techniques">

Mastering the controls in Heave Ho 2 is the difference between a smooth, satisfying run and endless, frustrating resets. The game translates simple inputs into complex physical momentum, allowing skilled players to perform impressive aerial maneuvers, climbing sequences, and precise throws.

| Action | Controller Input | Pro Technique |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Move & Rotate** | Left Analog Stick | Use tight circular motions for precise climbs; use wide sweeps to build swing momentum. |
| **Grip Left Hand** | Left Trigger / L Button | Hold to anchor; alternate with the right hand to climb vertical poles hand-over-hand. |
| **Grip Right Hand** | Right Trigger / R Button | Use as your primary reaching hand while keeping the left hand secured to a safe platform. |
| **Release Grip** | Release matching Trigger | Release at the peak of your swing's outward arc to maximize horizontal distance. |
| **Throw Ally** | Build swing, then release | The throwing player must release at a 45-degree angle to launch their ally upward. |
| **Catch Ally** | Hold trigger near flying ally | Open your catching hand early and keep your body still; do not chase the falling player. |

Advanced players can utilize momentum transfers to bypass difficult sections. For instance, when swinging at high speeds, releasing both hands simultaneously will launch your character in a high, arc-like trajectory. This is highly effective for crossing wide chasms, though it requires precise timing to land safely on small, distant platforms.

<Callout type="tip" title="Rhythm is Key">
When playing with a group, establish a vocal rhythm. Having one player call out 'one, two, swing, release' helps synchronize inputs and ensures everyone lets go at the optimal moment.
</Callout>
