<Section icon={Swords} title="Controls and Advanced Swinging Techniques">

Unlike traditional platformers, Heave Ho 2 does not feature a dedicated jump button. Instead, movement is entirely momentum-based and relies on the independent manipulation of your character's left and right hands. To successfully navigate the treacherous gaps, you must master the art of grabbing, rotating, and releasing at precisely the right moment.

| Action | PC Controller Input | Switch / Switch 2 Input | Tactical Execution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Move & Rotate** | Left Analog Stick | Left Analog Stick | Rotates the character's body around the active grip point. |
| **Grip Left Hand** | Left Trigger (LT) / L2 | L Button / ZL Trigger | Closes the left hand. Use to anchor yourself to walls or teammates. |
| **Grip Right Hand** | Right Trigger (RT) / R2 | R Button / ZR Trigger | Closes the right hand. Keep held when dangling over open space. |
| **Release Grip** | Release Matching Trigger | Release Matching Button | Opens the hand. Release at the peak of a swing to gain maximum distance. |

To help your team survive the trickiest sections of the **heave ho 2 demo**, practice these three foundational physics techniques. Coordinating these movements with your teammates is the key to preventing disastrous falls.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Anchor Strategy">
    Before any teammate attempts to swing across a gap, at least one player must act as a designated **Anchor**. This player should firmly grip a solid, non-slippery surface with both hands and remain completely still. Never let the anchor swing or release until the moving player has secured a new grip point.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Building the Human Chain">
    For wider chasms, players must link together. The anchor holds the wall, the second player grabs the anchor's waist or free hand, and subsequent players extend outward. This **Human Chain** can reach distant platforms. Always ensure the strongest player is at the base of the chain.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Precision Release">
    To launch a teammate, the holding player must swing them in a circular motion. Once maximum momentum is reached, the holding player must release their grip at the outward arc. The launched player must keep one hand open and ready to grab the target platform immediately upon impact.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Simultaneous Releases">
A common beginner mistake is having both players in a chain attempt to adjust their grip at the same time. Always communicate verbally before letting go of a teammate. If both players release, the entire chain will fall into the abyss.
</Callout>

</Section>
