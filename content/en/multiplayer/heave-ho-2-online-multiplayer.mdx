<Section icon={Settings} title="Setup Guide: How to Play Heave Ho 2 Online Multiplayer">

Setting up a session in **Heave Ho 2 online multiplayer** is designed to be user-friendly, but the process varies slightly depending on your chosen platform. The game uses a lobby-based system where a host creates a room and others join via a code or friend list invitation.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize the Game Lobby">
    Launch the game and navigate to the 'Multiplayer' menu. Select 'Online Play' and choose 'Host Game'. You will be prompted to select either the 'Campaign' (Co-op) or 'Showdown' (Versus) mode.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Lobby Privacy">
    Set your lobby to 'Friends Only' or 'Private' if you want to play with a specific group. If you choose Private, the game will generate a unique 6-digit Lobby Code to share with your teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Invite Your Teammates">
    On PC, use the Steam Overlay (Shift+Tab) to invite friends directly. On Nintendo Switch and Switch 2, use the in-game 'Invite Friends' button to pull up your console friend list.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Calibrate Controllers">
    Once all players have joined the lobby, every participant must press the grip buttons (L/R or Triggers) to confirm their presence. This ensures that everyone's input is correctly recognized before the level starts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Select a World and Start">
    The host selects one of the eight themed worlds, such as 'Flying High' or 'Spacebound Squad'. Once everyone hits 'Ready', the game will transition into the first level of that adventure.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Platform Requirement">
Nintendo Switch and Switch 2 players must have an active Nintendo Switch Online (NSO) subscription to access any online multiplayer features. Local GameShare, however, does not require a subscription.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Co-op Strategies for Online Success">

Playing online adds the challenge of communication lag. Unlike local co-op where you can shout at the person next to you, online play requires a more structured approach to movement. To clear the more difficult worlds like 'Transfolab' or 'Medieval Travels', your team needs to act as a single unit.

Successful teams often assign specific roles to players based on their position in the human chain. This prevents the "everyone release at once" disaster that leads to a total team fall.

| Player Role | Primary Responsibility | Best Position | Essential Technique |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **The Anchor** | Holds the safe surface | Rear of the chain | Never release until front is secure |
| **The Swinger** | Builds momentum | Middle of the chain | Synchronized circular rotation |
| **The Grabber** | Makes the next catch | Front of the chain | Precise hand-eye coordination |
| **The Courier** | Carries objects (Keys/Food) | Middle or Front | Short, safe handoffs |

**Key Tactical Considerations:**
- **Momentum Counting**: Use a 1-2-3 count for swings. This ensures everyone pulls and releases at the exact same moment.
- **Visual Indicators**: Pay attention to the hand icons on the screen. A closed fist indicates a secure grip, while an open hand means that player is free.
- **Recovery Anchors**: Always keep at least one player attached to a solid wall. If the front three players miss a jump, the anchor can pull them back.

<Callout type="success" title="Advanced Tip">
In 'Spacebound Squad', gravity is low. Use this to your advantage by creating extremely long chains. Because weight is less of a factor, a 4-player chain can span nearly half the screen, allowing the 'Grabber' to reach distant platforms with minimal swinging.
</Callout>

</Section>
