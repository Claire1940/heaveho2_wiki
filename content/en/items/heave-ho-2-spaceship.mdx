<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the heave ho 2 spaceship: Co-op Controls">

The centerpiece of World 3 is the cooperative vehicle sequence. The **heave ho 2 spaceship** is not controlled by a single pilot; instead, it features multiple interactive handles that require physical manipulation from the entire team. To move, rotate, and steer the ship, players must grab specific thruster levers and rotate their character arms in synchronized patterns. 

Without clear communication, the ship will spin wildly out of control, colliding with hazards and resetting your progress. Understanding your role on the ship is the first step toward a successful launch.

| Role | Required Input | Main Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Pilot (Left Thruster) | Analog stick rotation + Left grip | Controls left-side thrust and counter-clockwise rotation |
| Co-Pilot (Right Thruster) | Analog stick rotation + Right grip | Controls right-side thrust and clockwise rotation |
| Stabilizer | Alternating hand grips on hull | Keeps the ship balanced and prevents wild spins |
| Navigator | Free movement, active scanning | Grabs keys or targets while the ship is in motion |

To successfully navigate the tight corridors of Spacebound Squad, follow this step-by-step piloting sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure Your Handles">
    Every player must latch onto a designated handle on the spaceship. Ensure the left and right thruster handles are occupied by your most coordinated players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Synchronize the Thrust">
    To move forward in a straight line, both the left and right pilots must rotate their analog sticks in unison. Small, rhythmic circles work better than wild thrashing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Controlled Turns">
    To turn left, the right-side pilot should apply thrust while the left-side pilot holds steady. Reverse this process to execute precise right-hand turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Dock and Exit Safely">
    Once you reach the landing platform, stabilizers must reach out and grip the environment immediately. Do not release your spaceship grips until the ship is fully anchored.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Thruster Miscoordination">
If both thruster pilots pull in opposite directions without communicating, the spaceship will spin rapidly. Establish a clear voice leader to call out thrust timing, such as "pull left" or "both forward," to maintain a stable trajectory.
</Callout>
