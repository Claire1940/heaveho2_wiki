<Section icon={Swords} title="Stage-Specific Interactive Objects">

The campaign's eight themed worlds introduce a variety of **heave ho 2 items** that are native to their respective environments. These objects are not brought into the level by the player but are instead core components of the level's puzzle design. For instance, in the kitchen-themed "Chef's Orders" world, players must handle oversized ingredients and ketchup bottles, which possess unique weight and friction properties. Coordination is vital when moving these items, as their bulk can easily pull an unanchored player off a platform.

| World Name | Unique Stage Objects | Primary Function | Interaction Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flying High** | Ski Lifts, Gondolas | Timed transport | Keep one player anchored to the lift |
| **Manor Mysteries** | Hidden Keys | Unlocking stage exits | Use short handoffs to move keys |
| **Spacebound Squad** | Spaceships, Lasers | Navigation and Hazards | Pilot vehicles with synchronized pulls |
| **Chef's Orders** | Ingredients, Ketchup | Objective stacking | Move one bulky item at a time |
| **Medieval Travels** | King's Throne | Delicate transport | Balance the throne to prevent tipping |
| **Transfolab** | Interactive Devices | Puzzle manipulation | Assign one player to trigger switches |

In the "Medieval Travels" world, the King's Throne is perhaps the most challenging stage object to manage. It requires at least two players to maintain balance while the others provide the swinging momentum. If the throne tilts too far in one direction, the physics engine will cause it to tumble, often taking the entire team down with it.

<Callout type="warning" title="Physics Warning">
Interactive objects like the Pop Gun or Spaceship respond to the collective force of all players. If your team pulls in opposite directions, the object will stall or rotate uncontrollably.
</Callout>
