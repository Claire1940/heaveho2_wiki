<Section icon={Map} title="Detailed World Walkthroughs and Mechanics">

Navigating **Heave Ho 2 all levels** requires understanding the distinct personality of each world. The game does not just increase difficulty through tighter jumps; it adds layers of complexity by forcing players to interact with the environment in new ways. For instance, in "Manor Mysteries," you cannot simply reach the end; you must find a physical key hidden in the level and transport it safely to the exit. This changes the team dynamic, as the player holding the key has only one hand available for grabbing.

| World | Primary Hazard | Team Requirement | Difficulty Rating |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flying High** | Moving Lifts | Timing releases | ★★☆☆☆ |
| **Spacebound Squad** | No Gravity | Momentum control | ★★★★☆ |
| **Chef's Orders** | Bulky Objects | Balanced lifting | ★★★☆☆ |

In "Spacebound Squad," the physics engine shifts to accommodate zero gravity. Traditional swinging is replaced by "push-off" mechanics. If your team releases their grip simultaneously without a trajectory, you will drift aimlessly. Coordination here is higher than in any other world, making it a frequent roadblock for casual groups.

<Callout type="warning" title="Object Physics">
When carrying objects like the King's throne or giant burger ingredients, the weight of the item affects your swing speed. Do not attempt "Full Circles" while holding heavy items, as you will likely lose your grip.
</Callout>

In the final world, "Medieval Travels," the difficulty peaks with the throne-transport levels. You must keep the King upright while crossing gaps. If the throne tips over, the level resets. This requires at least two players to hold the base of the throne while a third player acts as the "swing lead" to find the next anchor point.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Human Chain Technique">

To clear the most difficult sections of **Heave Ho 2 all levels**, you must master the art of the human chain. This is the fundamental strategy for crossing gaps that are wider than a single character's reach. A successful chain is a living bridge that requires constant communication.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Anchor">
    The strongest player grips a solid, non-moving surface with both hands. This player must not let go until the front of the chain has secured a new position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Link">
    The second player grabs the feet or hands of the anchor. Subsequent players attach to each other, extending the chain toward the target platform.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Momentum">
    The players in the middle and front of the chain rotate their bodies in a synchronized rhythm. Use the "1-2-3-Swing" verbal cue to maximize the arc.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Launch and Catch">
    At the peak of the swing, the lead player extends a free hand toward the target. Once they grip the new surface, the rear anchor can release and swing forward to join the group.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Communication is Key">
Use simple, one-word commands like "Hold," "Swing," and "Release." In the Nintendo Switch 2 version, using GameChat is highly recommended for remote play to ensure timing is synchronized.
</Callout>

</Section>
