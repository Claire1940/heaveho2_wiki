<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Traversal and Team Coordination">

Success in **heave ho 2 worlds** is not about individual skill but about how well you function as a single physical unit. The "Human Chain" remains the most effective way to cross large gaps, but the sequel adds new layers to this classic mechanic.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish a Secure Anchor">
    At least one player must have a firm grip on a static environment object (like a wall or pole). This player should not move until the rest of the chain has secured the next landing point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build the Chain Rhythm">
    Connected players should rotate their analog sticks in the same direction simultaneously. This builds collective momentum, allowing the player at the end of the chain to reach much further than they could alone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Calculated Release">
    The "anchor" player or the "thrower" must communicate the release. Releasing at the peak of the outward swing converts rotational speed into horizontal distance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Catch and Reset">
    The flying player must keep one hand open. Once they grab the target, they become the new anchor, and the rest of the team swings across to join them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Beyond basic swinging, players can utilize unlockable support items to bypass difficult sections. These items are earned by completing optional objectives throughout the eight worlds.

| Support Item | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ima Rock** | Freezes player in midair | Creating a temporary platform over gaps |
| **Arm Spray** | Extends arm length | Reaching distant teammates or anchors |
| **Umbrella** | Slows downward descent | Precision landings on small platforms |
| **Cat Drone** | Automatic assist | Bypassing sections after multiple failures |

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Tip">
When carrying ingredients in **Chef's Orders**, do not try to throw them. Use "handoffs" where one player holds the object while another grabs it, ensuring the item is never unsupported in midair.
</Callout>
