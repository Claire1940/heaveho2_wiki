<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Physics and Controls for 100% Success">

To reach **heave ho 2 100 percent** status, you must master the game's unique two-hand control scheme. Unlike traditional platformers, there is no jump button. Every movement is a result of grip, rotation, and momentum. Mastery involves knowing exactly when to let go of the left trigger while keeping the right trigger held firm.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Anchor Strategy">
    Before any swing, ensure one player is firmly attached to a static surface. This player acts as the anchor. Never have the entire team release their grips simultaneously unless you are mid-flight toward a safe platform.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Building Momentum">
    Rotate your character in small circles to build speed. For longer chains, the anchored player should remain still while the dangling players coordinate their rotations to maximize the arc of the swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Precision Release">
    Release the grip button at the apex of your swing. The character will fly in the direction of their current trajectory. Aim slightly above your target to account for gravity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Rescue Grab">
    If a teammate falls, extend a free hand immediately. You can grab any part of their body to stop their momentum. Recoveries are essential for clearing levels without resetting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Advanced players use specific techniques to bypass difficult sections. These methods are crucial for beating the "Timer" challenges required for full completion.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Hand-Over-Hand Climbing">
    Alternate your left and right grips rapidly while rotating the stick upward to climb vertical poles and walls faster than standard movement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Human Whip">
    In a 4-player chain, the first player swings hard, and each subsequent player releases in sequence to launch the final player across massive gaps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Momentum Transfer">
    Use stage objects like **Ski Lifts** or **Gondolas** to gain initial speed, then release at the highest point to clear half the level in one jump.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Control Tip">
Check the on-screen hand indicators frequently. In chaotic 4-player sessions, it is easy to forget which trigger controls which hand. The indicators will glow when a grip is successfully established.
