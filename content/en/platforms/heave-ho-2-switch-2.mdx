<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Controls and Swinging Physics">

Success in this game depends entirely on mastering the momentum-based control scheme. Unlike traditional platformers, there is no dedicated jump button. Your character consists of a round body and two extremely long, elastic arms. You must manually control each hand to grip, hold, and release surfaces or teammates. 

The analog stick controls the rotation and direction of your arms. By grabbing a surface with one hand, you can swing your body in circular patterns to build up speed. Releasing your grip at the peak of your swing launches you through the air.

| Input Command | Action Performed | Tactical Gameplay Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Analog Stick** | Move and Rotate | Rotates arms and body around the active grip point |
| **Left Trigger (L/ZL)** | Grip Left Hand | Closes left hand around surfaces, objects, or allies |
| **Right Trigger (R/ZR)** | Grip Right Hand | Closes right hand around surfaces, objects, or allies |
| **Release Trigger** | Let Go Grip | Opens the hand to release hold and transition momentum |

<Callout type="tip" title="Alternating Grip Technique">
When climbing vertical walls or moving along ropes, never release both triggers at the same time. Always secure your next grip point with a free hand before releasing the trailing hand to prevent falling.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Swinging Progression">

Crossing massive gaps and dodging hazards requires structured team execution. Use this step-by-step progression to coordinate throws and build stable human chains.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish a Secure Anchor">
    Before any player swings, the trailing player must secure a firm grip on a solid, non-slippery platform. This anchor player should remain completely still and hold both triggers down to guarantee stability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Human Chain">
    The second player grabs the hips or hands of the anchor player. Additional players then connect to the bottom of the chain. Always verify that each link in the chain is holding the correct player before moving.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Generate Rotational Momentum">
    All connected players must rotate their analog sticks in the same direction. Start with small, controlled swings, gradually increasing the arc. Avoid wild, uncontrolled rotations that can cause players to lose their grip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Launch and Catch">
    The lead player at the bottom of the chain must extend an open hand toward the target platform. The anchor player or intermediate links release their grip at the highest point of the upward swing to launch the lead player across the gap.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Pull the Team Up">
    Once the lead player safely grabs the destination platform, they become the new anchor. The remaining players can then swing across or climb up the newly established anchor chain to complete the crossing safely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Chain Fatigue Risk">
Longer chains of three or four players generate immense physics strain. If players rotate in opposing directions, the force will break the chain, sending the lower players falling into the abyss.
</Callout>

</Section>
