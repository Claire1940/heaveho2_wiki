<Section icon={Swords} title="Movement Controls and Swinging Techniques">

Success in this physics-driven environment requires abandoning traditional platformer logic. There is no dedicated jump button. Instead, your character consists of a circular head and two independently controlled elastic arms. Movement relies entirely on rotating your body, securing a grip with the correct hand, and transferring momentum between surfaces and teammates.

| Action | Controller Input | Keyboard Input | Core Gameplay Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Move & Rotate | Left Analog Stick | WASD / Arrow Keys | Rotates character arms and body around the active grip point |
| Grip Left Hand | Left Trigger (LT/L) | Left Shift / A | Closes the left hand around a surface, object, or teammate |
| Grip Right Hand | Right Trigger (RT/R) | Spacebar / D | Closes the right hand around a surface, object, or teammate |
| Release Grip | Release Trigger | Release Key | Opens the corresponding hand to launch or drop from an anchor |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish an Anchor">
    Before attempting any swing, ensure at least one player is firmly attached to a solid, non-slippery environmental surface. This player serves as the anchor for the rest of the team.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Chain">
    Have consecutive players grab the body, hands, or head of the anchored player. Extend the human chain outward toward the target platform to bridge wide gaps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Generate Rotational Momentum">
    All connected players must rotate their analog sticks in a synchronized circular motion. Build speed gradually rather than rushing the swing to maintain control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Release">
    The launching player must release their connecting grip at the peak outward point of the swing. Keep the landing hand open and pointed toward the destination.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Secure the Catch">
    As the flying player approaches the target platform, hold the corresponding grip trigger immediately upon contact to secure a stable hold.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Momentum Control">
Avoid releasing both triggers simultaneously when attempting to swing. Always maintain a single handhold until your trajectory is established, converting rotational speed into linear distance.
