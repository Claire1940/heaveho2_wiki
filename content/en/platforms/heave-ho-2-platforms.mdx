<Section icon={Target} title="How to Set Up Controllers on PC">

While keyboard controls are technically supported, the developers strongly recommend playing with a controller. The physics-based mechanics require precise analog control over your character's arms and grip. Using an analog stick makes aiming your swings significantly easier than using digital keyboard keys.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Connect Your Controller">
    Plug in your Xbox, PlayStation, or Nintendo Switch controller via USB, or pair it using Bluetooth before launching Steam.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable Steam Input">
    Open Steam Settings, navigate to the Controller tab, and ensure that Steam Input is enabled for your specific controller type.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Verify Game Layout">
    Launch the game, enter the options menu, and confirm that the trigger buttons correctly map to your character's left and right hands.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once configured, your left trigger controls your left hand, and your right trigger controls your right hand. The analog stick directs your body's momentum. Master this layout to avoid plunging into the void during complex level transitions.

<Callout type="info" title="Controller Calibration">
If you experience stick drift or unresponsive grabbing, adjust the deadzone settings in Steam's controller configuration panel rather than the in-game options.
</Callout>

</Section>
