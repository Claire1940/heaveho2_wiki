<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering the Physics and Controls">

The learning curve in **heave ho 2 ps5** revolves around mastering the momentum of your character's swing. Unlike traditional platformers, you do not have a jump button. Instead, you move by rotating your arms and grabbing onto geometry with the L2 and R2 buttons. Coordination is key, especially when you are part of a "human chain" trying to reach a distant goal.

| Control Action | PS5 Button | Mechanic Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Hand Grip** | L2 Trigger | Holds onto any solid surface or teammate |
| **Right Hand Grip** | R2 Trigger | Holds onto any solid surface or teammate |
| **Arm Rotation** | Left Analog | Rotates the body to build centrifugal force |
| **Let Go / Throw** | Release L2/R2 | Releases the grip to launch into the air |
| **Emote / Scream** | Circle Button | Triggers a hilarious vocal reaction |

One of the new mechanics in the sequel is the "Power Throw." When two players hold onto each other, they can synchronize their rotation to launch the bottom player with double the normal velocity. This is required for several "S-Rank" time trials in the later worlds.

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch Your Grip">
Holding onto a teammate for too long will cause your character's hands to sweat, indicated by a shaking animation. If you don't find a solid ledge soon, you will automatically let go.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Your First Level">

If you are new to the series, the first few levels of **heave ho 2 ps5** can be overwhelming. The physics feel "heavy" and counter-intuitive at first. Follow these steps to build your confidence and master the basic movement loop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Test Your Grip">
    Start by alternating between L2 and R2. Get a feel for how your character "hangs" from a single hand versus two hands. Notice the arc of your body when you rotate the analog stick.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Building Momentum">
    Find a flat ceiling or a long horizontal bar. Grab with one hand and rotate in a full circle. Release at the 2 o'clock position to launch yourself forward and upward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Hand-Off">
    In co-op, practice grabbing a teammate's hand while they are stationary. Once connected, have the top player swing the bottom player like a pendulum to reach a far ledge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Precision Landing">
    As you fly through the air, keep both L2 and R2 held down. This ensures that you will grab the first thing you touch, preventing you from bouncing off a platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>
