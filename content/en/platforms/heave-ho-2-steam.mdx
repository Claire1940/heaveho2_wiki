<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Physics-Based Controls and Movement">

Movement in **heave ho 2 steam** is fundamentally different from traditional platformers. You do not have a jump button; instead, you rely on the rotation of your arms and the grip of your hands. Mastering the cadence of grabbing and releasing is the difference between a successful crossing and a tragic fall into the abyss.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Independent Grips">
    - **Left Trigger**: Controls the left hand.
    - **Right Trigger**: Controls the right hand.
    - **Strategy**: Alternate grips to climb vertical surfaces hand-over-hand.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Momentum Building">
    - **Analog Stick**: Rotates your body.
    - **Technique**: Swing in a circular motion to build centrifugal force.
    - **Release**: Let go at the 2 o'clock or 10 o'clock position for max distance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team Chains">
    - **Connection**: Grab a friend's hand or body.
    - **Anchor**: One player must remain attached to a solid surface.
    - **Swing**: The anchor holds firm while the chain builds momentum.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To excel in the 72 co-op levels, players must understand the "Anchor-Swing-Catch" cycle. This involves one player securing the team, the middle players providing length, and the lead player reaching for the next platform.

| Action | Control Input | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Climb** | Alternate L/R Triggers | Reach higher with the free hand before releasing the lower grip. |
| **Throw** | Build swing + Release | Thrown players should keep one hand open to catch the target. |
| **Catch** | Hold Trigger on contact | Open your hand early to increase the catch window during a fall. |
| **Rotate** | Rotate Left Stick | Small circles for precision, large sweeps for long jumps. |
| **Stay Still** | Hold both Triggers | Useful when waiting for moving platforms like gondolas. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Advanced Grip Technique">
When climbing thin poles, keep your body as close to the pole as possible. Rotating too wide can cause you to lose momentum and slip during the hand-over-hand transition.
</Callout>
