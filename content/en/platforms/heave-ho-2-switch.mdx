<Section icon={Target} title="Controls and Swinging Techniques">

Unlike traditional platformers, Heave Ho 2 does not feature a jump button. Instead, movement relies entirely on physics, momentum, and independent control of your character's two hands. Your character is essentially a round head with two long, elastic arms. You must rotate your arms using the analog stick and grab surfaces using the left and right triggers. 

Mastering this control layout is the key to navigating the game's increasingly difficult environments. If you release both triggers at the wrong time, your character will plummet into the abyss, often taking your entire team down with you.

| Action | Controller Input | Mechanics & Tips |
|---|---|---|
| **Move & Rotate** | Left Analog Stick | Rotates your arms and body around your current grip point. |
| **Grip Left Hand** | L / ZL Button | Closes your left hand around a surface, object, or teammate. |
| **Grip Right Hand** | R / ZR Button | Closes your right hand around a surface, object, or teammate. |
| **Release Hand** | Release Trigger | Opens the corresponding hand to let go of your current anchor. |
| **Climb Wall** | Alternate Triggers | Grab, rotate, grab with the other hand, and release the lower hand. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Trigger Coordination">
A common beginner mistake is releasing the wrong trigger when trying to move hand-over-hand. Always verify which hand is currently anchoring you to the wall before letting go of a button.
</Callout>

To help your team cross wide gaps, you must learn how to build momentum and form human chains. Follow these steps to execute a perfect team throw:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Anchor">
    Have the strongest or most reliable player grab a solid, non-slippery piece of the environment with both hands. This player serves as the anchor and must not let go under any circumstances.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Form the Chain">
    The second player should grab the anchor player's body or free arm. Additional players can join the chain, extending the group's reach across the empty space.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Momentum">
    All connected players must move their analog sticks in unison, swinging the chain back and forth. Use gradual, rhythmic movements to build up a wide, sweeping arc.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Launch and Catch">
    At the peak of the swing, the player at the very end of the chain must release their grip on the teammate behind them, launching themselves toward the target platform. Keep your hands open to grab the new ledge immediately upon contact.
  </Step>
</Steps>
